Timeline

1916 - Born in Purbalingga

General Sudirman, whose real name is Raden Sudirman, was born on January 24, 1916, in Purbalingga, Central Java Province.

General Sudirman's parents were Mr. Karsid Kartawiraji, a sugar factory worker in Kalibagor, Banyumas, and Mrs. Siyem who was descended from Wedana Rembang. He has one brother, Muhammad Samingan. His wife is Alifah and with him, he has 7 children.

1936 - Become a teacher

After graduating from Wirotomo, Sudirman studied for one year at the Kweekschool (teacher school) run by Muhammadiyah in Surakarta but quit due to lack of funds. In 1936, he returned to Cilacap to teach at a Muhammadiyah elementary school, having been trained by his teachers at Wirotomo.

Sudirman continued to teach, before joining the Japanese-sponsored Defenders of the Homeland as a battalion commander in Banyumas. In this position he put down a rebellion by his fellow soldiers, but was later interned in Bogor.

As a teacher, Sudirman taught his students lessons on morality using examples from the lives of the prophets and traditional wayang stories. One of his students later recalled that Sudirman was an even-handed and patient teacher who would mix humour and nationalism in his lessons

1945 - Japanese occupation

When World War II broke out in Europe, it was thought that Japan, already moving closer to mainland China, would attempt to invade the Indies. In response, the Dutch colonial government – which had previously restricted military training to natives – began teaching people how to deal with air raids. Following up on this, the Dutch then formed an Air Attack Preparation team.

Sudirman, who was respected by the community, was asked to lead the team in Cilacap. Apart from teaching local residents about safety procedures to deal with air attacks, Sudirman also established monitoring posts throughout the area. He and Holland also handled aircraft dropping material to simulate bombing; it aims to increase the response rate.

1945 - National revolution

As a newly independent country and did not yet have a professional military, on October 5, 1945, Soekarno issued a decree establishing the People's Security Army (TKR, now known as the Indonesian National Army). Most of the personnel are former KNIL soldiers, while the senior officers are from PETA and Heihō.

1945 - Become a General

On November 12, 1945, in the first meeting of the TKR, Sudirman was elected as the leader of the TKR after going through a two-stage deadlocked vote. In the third stage, Oerip collected 21 votes, while Sudirman was ahead with 22 votes; the commanders of the Sumatran division all voted for Sudirman. Sudirman, then 29 years old, was surprised by the election results and offered to relinquish the position to Oerip, but the meeting participants would not allow it. Oerip, who had lost control of the meeting even before voting began, was pleased that he was no longer in charge of TKR. Sudirman continued to appoint Oerip as chief of staff. In accordance with his new position, Sudirman was promoted to General.

Death

In early August Sudirman approached Sukarno and asked him to continue the guerrilla war; Sudirman did not expect the Dutch to abide by the Roem-Royen Agreement, based on the failings of the previous agreements. Sukarno disagreed, which was a blow to Sudirman. When Sudirman threatened to resign his post, blaming the government's inconsistency for his tuberculosis and Oerip's death in November 1948, Sukarno threatened to do so as well. As he thought that such a resignation would have a destabilising effect, Sudirman stayed his hand and a Java-wide cease fire came into effect on 11 August 1949.
